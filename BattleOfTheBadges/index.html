Once a year the Oro-Medonte Fire Fighters and the County of Simcoe Paramedic Services (CSPS) go head-to-head in a charity hockey game to raise money for different organizations. People from the community and surrounding areas come together to cheer on the teams and to help support Camp Ooch. It is a fun, enjoyable and meaningful hockey game.

The Oro-Medonte firefighters and CSPS take great pride in giving back to the community. Funds raised at this year’s event will benefit Camp Oochigeas, a privately-funded, volunteer-based organization that provides a unique opportunity for children living with cancer-related issues.

Camp Oochigeas is much more than a summer camp. Camp Oochigeas offers year-round programs for children affected by childhood cancer at our site in Muskoka, at SickKids Hospital, at our Ooch Downtown facility in Toronto and in the community.

Through these programs Camp Oochigeas provides fun and meaningful experiences to approximately 800 children affected by cancer each year at no cost to their families. This is all made possible by the generous support of our donors and volunteers. Learn how you can get involved.
